INTRO

FORETHOUGHT

Hello, reader. I have a question for you. Have you ever dreamed of a made-up place more than once? I don't mean a location that's familiar to your waking world; I mean a setting that is comprehensible enough to recall in your mind and persistent enough to pervade through multiple days, weeks, or years of different dreams. I've found it intriguing that despite the amorphous nature of my dreams - the seemingly incessant shape-shifting of faces and the disorienting lack of contingency - I would often find my dream-self wandering through the same hallways I've never walked through and seeing the same buildings I've never seen. Does this happen to you too?

With no scientific merit and solely because I find it intriguing, I'd like to think that the subconscious, creative minds in some people are constantly recontextualizing the spaces they've been to, dissecting the miscellaneous aspects they like and reconstructing new worlds in their heads. Similarly to how a musician is subconsciously influenced to emulate the musical aspects they find favorable in other music, a world-builder makes worlds that are derived from all the little favorable things they subconsciously internalized from their environments, real and imagined.

If you share this phenomenon with me, you may be a world-builder yourself. But, just as there's a distinction between being an avid music listener and a musician, there's something that differentiates a world-dreamer from a world-builder. It's the act of reaching down into the subconscious and pulling its contents into the perceptible world - taking all these disparate alogical ideas and weaving them together into something with meaning, consequence, and reason. That's what this page is about. Welcome to Meyhemn.

GEOGRAPHY

THE PRINCIPALITY OF MIDBURLAND

"Midburland? The north is real nice - they got fertile fields, sturdy roofs, and flushing shitters. Down here you're lucky to have walls thick enough to protect your hide from the bandits and beasts - the real ugly sons-a-bitches that reside out there in the swamplands." -Lenny Shagrits, Boghelm resident

North Midburland is a relatively peaceful continent. The same cannot be said for the South. The land is best understood as three disparate lands strung together. Northern Midburland is a wide, lush continent with a diverse ecosystem and thriving population of humanoids and creatures. Southern Midburland is a land of steep, jagged, untraversable cliffs dispersed by lowly swamps and wetlands rife with unfavorable wildlife, speckled with shantytowns and fishing villages. The two lands are attached by a terrible stretch of dense forest known as the Tether. Nobody lives in the Tether, at least not for very long. The coastal oceans of Midburland are plagued by frequent storms and tumultuous waves, making conventional sailing ill-advised.

BOGHELM

Boghelm is where your journey begins. A meager fishing village built on a strip of grasslands amongst a swamp teeming with fish of all shapes and sizes. The Boathouse: The swampy forest abruptly gives way to a small sandy shore, atop which perches a pier and its accompanying boathouse. The mahogany wood looks sturdy and ornate, sharply contrasting with most of the shoddy infrastructure you're used to seeing in Boghelm. The pier houses a few skiffs and two larger ships, and on the right is an elevated boathouse with intricately carved windows and a thatch roof. Through the window, you can see a group of people and racks stacked with canoes.
